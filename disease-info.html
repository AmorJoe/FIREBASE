Skin Conditions Library

Comprehensive guide to dermatological conditions supported by our AI model.

Inflammatory

Eczema

A condition that makes your skin red and itchy. Also known as atopic dermatitis, it's common in children but can occur at any age. It's chronic and tends to flare periodically.

Malignant

Melanoma

The most serious type of skin cancer. It develops in the cells (melanocytes) that produce melanin. Early awareness of the ABCDE rules (Asymmetry, Border, Color, Diameter, Evolving) is vital.

Chronic

Atopic Dermatitis

The most common form of eczema. It's a condition that causes dry, itchy and inflamed skin. It often starts in childhood and can be associated with asthma or hay fever.

Skin Cancer

Basal Cell Carcinoma

A type of skin cancer that begins in the basal cells. It often appears as a slightly transparent bump on the skin, though it can take other forms. It usually occurs on sun-exposed areas.

Benign

Melanocytic Nevi

Commonly known as moles, these are clusters of pigmented cells. Most are harmless, but monitoring them for changes in size, shape, or color is important for early skin cancer detection.

Benign

Keratosis-like Lesions

A category of benign lesions that include seborrheic keratoses, solar lentigines, and lichen-planus-like keratoses. These are non-cancerous but can sometimes resemble other conditions.

Autoimmune

Psoriasis & Lichen

Psoriasis causes scaly patches, most commonly on knees and elbows. Lichen planus can cause swelling and irritation in the skin, hair, nails and mucous membranes.

Benign Growth

Seborrheic Keratoses

One of the most common noncancerous skin growths in older adults. It usually appears as a brown, black or light tan growth on the face, chest, shoulders or back.

Fungal

Fungal Infections

Conditions caused by fungi, including Tinea (Ringworm) and Candidiasis. These often present as itchy, red, circular rashes or white patches in moist areas of the body.

Viral

Viral Infections

Includes common warts, molluscum contagiosum, and other viral skin conditions. These are caused by viruses like HPV and are often contagious through skin-to-skin contact.
